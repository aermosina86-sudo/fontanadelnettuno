## Introduction

This project was developed within the framework of the KE4H course at the University of Bologna. Its aim is to explore how Knowledge Graphs and Large Language Models can be combined to enrich existing cultural heritage data.

The selected topic is the **Fontana del Nettuno in Bologna**, one of the most recognizable monuments in the city. The project uses the ArCo Knowledge Graph and SPARQL queries to investigate how this cultural property is represented in structured data. It then uses Large Language Models, such as ChatGPT and Gemini, to propose possible RDF triples that could enrich the existing knowledge graph.

## Project Objective

The objective of this project is to identify possible information gaps in the ArCo representation of the Fontana del Nettuno and to test whether LLMs can help generate reliable candidate knowledge for RDF enrichment.

## Research Question

**How can Large Language Models support the enrichment of ArCo RDF descriptions for the Fontana del Nettuno in Bologna, and how reliable are their outputs when compared with SPARQL results and human verification?**
