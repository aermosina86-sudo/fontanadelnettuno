## Why Fontana del Nettuno?

The **Fontana del Nettuno** was selected as the topic of this project because it is one of the most recognizable cultural landmarks in Bologna. Located in the historic city centre, the fountain has strong artistic, civic, and symbolic value. It is not only a monument, but also an important part of Bologna’s urban identity.

This makes it a useful case study for a project on cultural heritage knowledge graphs. A well-known monument such as the Fontana del Nettuno should ideally be represented in a structured and semantically rich way. However, knowledge graphs may still contain gaps, limited descriptions, or missing links between entities, subjects, creators, places, and historical context.

## Project Focus

This project focuses on how the Fontana del Nettuno is represented in the **ArCo Knowledge Graph**, the official ontology network and RDF dataset for Italian cultural heritage. Through SPARQL queries, the project investigates what information is already present and what information may be missing or incomplete.

The project then uses Large Language Models, especially **ChatGPT** and **Gemini**, to test whether they can help propose new RDF triples for enriching the existing knowledge graph.

## Why This Topic Is Suitable for KG Enrichment

The Fontana del Nettuno is suitable for this project because it allows us to investigate different types of semantic information, such as:

- the cultural property itself;
- its label and possible alternative names;
- its subject or iconographic meaning;
- its connection to Bologna;
- its creators or related agents;
- possible missing contextual information.

The goal is not to replace the ArCo data, but to propose possible enrichment triples based on SPARQL exploration, LLM-assisted generation, and human verification.

## Objectives

The main objectives are:

1. To explore the ArCo Knowledge Graph using SPARQL.
2. To identify the most relevant ArCo resource for the Fontana del Nettuno.
3. To detect possible information gaps in the RDF description.
4. To use ChatGPT and Gemini to propose candidate knowledge.
5. To compare the outputs of the two LLMs.
6. To create RDF triples that could enrich the existing representation.
